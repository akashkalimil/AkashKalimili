Intertial Navigation System with Wireless Communication and Data Processing with a FPGA

Using an accelerometer as a source of data, I designed and built analog signal conditioning circuitry to optimize the signal for digitization and serialization. After building ADC circuitry and Serialization Circuitry, I ran many diagnostic tests for baud rate and data loss with oscilloscopes to make sure I wasn't over sampling or undersampling. I used an RN-41 Bluetooth module. I used a microcontroller (Ardiuno) to program program protocols on the flash memory of the chip. On the recieving end of the connection, an FPGA (Nexys 5) was hooked up to the reciever. Using Xilinx , I implemented integration algorithms that I created. My solution for integration drift was numerous trials to calculate constants. At the end of the year, I presented this project at the annual TJStar Convention (Large event where research projects are presented to the school community. I worked with 6800 series Motorola Microprocessor and learned microprocessor architecture. I also designed and programmed the processor in assembly for a specific function. Other components of this project include ROMs, Counters, Logic Gates. During Advanced Audio Electronics course, used previous microprocessor circuit for DSP. Later on, I also worked with a gate array for DSP. The image below is a schematic, I designed, of the original processor circuit w/o any additional components which I have later created to act as supplements for the original design.

In Advanced Analog Electronics, I worked with operational amplifiers, transistors, and RC filters to design a decent quality audio amplifier. I then worked with PSpice and Circuit Lab to design a circuit that modulates multiple signals with one signal path and respectively demodulates them. Using configurations such as diode detectors, I developed AM modulation and demodulation circuits.

In Advanced Digital Electronics, I worked with inverters, and gates, or gates, nor gates to design an application with a multiplexed led display. I designed with up/down counters, decoders, and jfet transistors. I also used ROMs for my application and I had a rom for music storage. I developed with DACs for a source of audio.

In Advanced Audio Electronics, I worked with ADCs and DACs for DSP of an analog signal. At first I was working with discrete circuitry, but then I started working with FPGAs and CPLDs for the DSP component of the circuit. I also studied discrete fourier transform during the course.
